# Mune — Company context

The shared reference file every command should read before producing or reviewing content. If a piece of content contradicts anything here, it's wrong — no matter how well-written.

## What Mune is

Mune is a place people can write with a chatbot about anything — including the thoughts they have at 2 AM that they don't want to share, can't place, and don't want judged. The chatbot helps users make sense of what they're thinking and feeling, and can turn that into a journal entry on request.

The whole product is free. It is end-to-end encrypted. Privacy is a core part of the product, not a line in the copy.

## What Mune is not

- Not therapy. Not a replacement for therapy.
- Not a diagnosis tool.
- Not a "wellness app" with streaks, gamification, or "keep your mindfulness streak alive" notifications.
- Not a persona or character. The chatbot doesn't have a cute name, a cartoon avatar, or a personality arc.
- Not a social network. There is no profile, no feed, no followers, no public journal.
- Not freemium. There is no paid tier dangling features behind a wall.

Any copy that implies any of the above is wrong. Correct it.

## Who it's for

The person who:

- Writes notes in Apple Notes at 2 AM and deletes them before morning.
- Has a therapist (or doesn't) and still has things they don't say out loud.
- Distrusts apps that say "your data is safe with us" because they've read the privacy policy.
- Wants help structuring a thought without being sold a "journey."
- Would rather not have their name tied to a database of their worst moments.

Write to this person. Not to "mental health consumers." Not to "Gen Z." Not to "wellness-curious professionals."

## How the product actually works (plain facts to draw from)

- Chatbot interface. You write. It responds.
- Responses help you explore, clarify, and structure what you're thinking — not fix it, not cheer you up.
- You can ask the chatbot to turn the conversation into a journal entry.
- End-to-end encryption: data is encrypted on the device before it leaves it. The server cannot read your conversations.
- No account tied to legal identity required.
- Free. No pricing page.

When writing about the product, prefer concrete affordances over abstract benefits. "Ask for a journal entry" beats "get personalized insights."

## Positioning

**Category we're in** — private writing tool. Not "AI therapy app." Not "mental wellness platform." Not "journaling app" (too small; most journaling apps are about streaks). Private writing tool that can help.

**The enemy** (what the product rejects):

- The identity tax — the expectation that to get help, you hand over your name.
- Performative wellness — apps that turn inner life into content to share.
- Branded empathy — the chatbot that calls you "friend" and sends you hearts.
- Feature creep — streaks, mood tracking as gamification, community features.

**Preferred framing**:

- "A place to write" over "a mental health app."
- "Make sense of" over "process," "heal," or "work through."
- "Encrypted on your device" over "privacy-first" or "secure."
- "Free" stated plainly, not "forever free" or "always free."
- "Chatbot" is fine — we don't need a euphemism. Don't rebrand it as "companion" or "AI friend."

## Claims — what we can and cannot say

### Can say (factually accurate)

- End-to-end encrypted.
- Data is encrypted on your device before it leaves it.
- We cannot read your conversations.
- No account tied to your identity required.
- Completely free.
- Works at any hour, with any thought.
- Can turn a conversation into a journal entry.

### Cannot say

- "Replaces therapy," "better than therapy," or any direct therapy comparison.
- "Treats," "cures," "diagnoses," or any clinical outcome.
- "The only private" anything (superlatives without evidence).
- "Guaranteed," "proven to reduce anxiety," or any outcome guarantee.
- "Understands you" or claims about AI empathy/emotional intelligence.
- "100% secure" (no system is) — say "end-to-end encrypted" instead.
- "Your data never leaves your device" — it does leave (encrypted); it just can't be read by us.
- Testimonials, quotes, or user stories unless the user has explicitly consented AND we've confirmed with Fredrik.

### Required disclaimers (non-negotiable)

When content discusses mental health topics directly (suicide, self-harm, crisis, severe symptoms):

- Include a plain, short line directing the reader to a crisis resource in their country, placed near the top or in a visible place.
- Do not position Mune as the appropriate tool in a crisis. It isn't.
- Suggested language: *"If you're in crisis, Mune isn't the right place. Please contact a crisis line in your country — they're trained for this."*

For any clinical-adjacent claim that slipped in, flag it. Don't just soften it.

## Competitive landscape (for reference, not for mentioning by name in copy)

- **Journaling apps** (Day One, Journey): about streaks and memory. Different category.
- **AI therapy apps** (Woebot, Wysa): make clinical claims Mune deliberately doesn't make.
- **Chatbot companions** (Replika, Character.ai): persona-driven; Mune is explicitly not a persona.
- **Notes apps** (Apple Notes, Obsidian): private but not conversational.

Mune sits in a gap: the privacy of Notes with the help of a conversation, without the persona and without the clinical positioning.

Do not mention competitors by name in public marketing copy unless Fredrik has explicitly approved it. Comparative copy is a legal risk and a voice risk (sounds insecure).

## Founder voice

Most long-form content (blog posts, founder essays, personal social) is written from Fredrik's first-person perspective. Maintain "I" where it's been used; don't shift to "we" mid-piece.

Brand/product pages and transactional copy use "we" (the company) and "you" (the reader). Avoid "our community," "our users," or "our members."

## Languages

Primary: English.

Fredrik is Norwegian. If content will be translated or localized, flag that the voice (quiet, physical, anti-performance) should be preserved — not rebuilt from English marketing scratch by a translator who defaults to SaaS-speak.
