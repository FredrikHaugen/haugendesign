# Voice examples — annotated

Real passages from Fredrik's writing for Mune. Use these as pattern-matching targets. When you're unsure whether a draft sounds like Mune, read these first and compare.

## Source: "The Notes I Kept Deleting" (founder blog post)

### Opening — scene, not hook

> It was somewhere around 2 AM – phone on low brightness, thumb hovering over a half-written sentence in a notes app. Not even a real sentence. More like a confession that kept stopping halfway, like my brain was afraid of its own next word.

**What's working:**
- No headline formula. No "Are you tired of…?" No statistic.
- Physical details (low brightness, thumb hovering, half-written).
- The metaphor ("brain was afraid of its own next word") is specific and embodied, not abstract.
- "More like a confession that kept stopping halfway" — the honest hedge as reframe.

**What this tells us:** Mune long-form opens with a scene the reader recognizes, not a value prop.

---

### The three-beat list + obsessive routine

> * Search the internet for something that might help.
> * Open Apple Notes.
> * Write a few lines.
> * Read them back and feel the embarrassment.
> * Select all.
> * Delete.
> * Repeat.

**What's working:**
- Each line is a concrete action. No adjectives.
- Named brand ("Apple Notes") sharpens the picture.
- Ends on "Repeat" — the loop becomes the point without being named as "a loop."

**What this tells us:** When describing behavior, use bare verbs. Don't narrate the emotion on top — the structure carries it.

---

### Performing composure vs. actual safety

> It wasn't that I didn't have anyone to talk to. I did. I even started seeing a therapist. I showed up. I sat in the chair. I did the responsible thing.
>
> But there were still things I didn't say.

**What's working:**
- Three-beat list ("I showed up. I sat in the chair. I did the responsible thing.") does the emotional work.
- The reframe that follows is flat and declarative: "But there were still things I didn't say."
- Acknowledges the obvious counter ("why not just see a therapist?") and walks through it physically.

**What this tells us:** When the reader might object, name the objection plainly, then answer it with specifics — not argument.

---

### Fear described physically

> Because the fear wasn't logical. It was physical. It sat in my throat and made everything come out smaller than it really was.

**What's working:**
- "Sat in my throat" — the body, not the feeling.
- "Made everything come out smaller than it really was" — precise mechanism, not a cliché.
- Short sentences with hard periods.

**What this tells us:** Never describe emotion at the level of "I felt anxious." Always drop to the body or to the behavior that gives the emotion away.

---

### The repetition move for emphasis

> I was scared of being judged. Even by someone whose job was not to judge. Even by someone trained to handle it. Even by someone who would probably nod and say the right words.

**What's working:**
- Three "even by" clauses escalating.
- No bold, no italics. Structure carries emphasis.
- The final clause ("probably nod and say the right words") undercuts the implied reassurance without sneering at it.

**What this tells us:** When you want to emphasize, repeat with variation. Don't reach for formatting.

---

### The "not X, but Y" move, load-bearing

> A place I could speak freely and honestly without paying the identity tax. A place where privacy wasn't a marketing line, but the default. A place that didn't ask me to clean up my mess before it would let me hold it.

**What's working:**
- Three parallel "A place…" clauses building the shape of the thing.
- "Not a marketing line, but the default" — the signature reframe.
- "Clean up my mess before it would let me hold it" — anti-therapy-speak phrasing that still earns the emotional content.

**What this tells us:** The landing page hero or founder thesis should feel earned by the paragraph before it, not dropped in as a slogan.

---

### The compressed positioning

> Anonymity, not as an aesthetic, but as a relief. Privacy, not as a promise, but as architecture. Thoughtful support, without the persona.

**What's working:**
- Three compressed beats, all "not X, but Y" or "X without Y."
- Each line could be landing page hero copy.
- Abstract words (anonymity, privacy, support) earned by the paragraphs that precede them.

**What this tells us:** These are the closest to pull-quote-able brand lines in all of Mune's writing. Don't try to one-up them with something "punchier." They're already compressed.

---

### The closing — aphoristic, load-bearing

> After deleting my last "2 AM note", building my own help felt like less work than searching and pretending it didn't matter.

**What's working:**
- No call-to-action. No "And that's why I built Mune, the revolutionary…"
- Ends on the private calculation that led to the product, not the product itself.
- The reader arrives at the pitch by reading between the lines.

**What this tells us:** Long-form Mune pieces don't close with a CTA. They close with a sentence that leaves the reader holding something.

## Anti-patterns — what the voice is not

Examples of sentences that would be wrong for Mune. If a draft sounds like any of these, rewrite.

**Too cheerful / too SaaS:**
- "Welcome to Mune — your personal space for mental wellness!"
- "Start your journey to a calmer mind today."
- "Mune is the all-in-one AI companion for your emotional wellbeing."

**Too therapy-coded:**
- "A safe space to lean into your vulnerability."
- "Mune holds space for your healing journey."
- "Process your feelings with compassion."

**Too founder-hustle:**
- "I built Mune to disrupt the mental health space."
- "We're on a mission to democratize emotional support."
- "At the end of the day, everyone deserves a tool that 10x's their self-awareness."

**Too generic-adjective:**
- "A warm, supportive, thoughtful, private space to write."
- "Beautifully designed. Deeply personal. Always there for you."

**Too performative-vulnerable:**
- "Let me be real with you — we all struggle."
- "This is my truth."
- "Raw, unfiltered, honest — that's what Mune stands for."

If you catch yourself reaching for any of these, stop. Go read the three compressed beats again.
